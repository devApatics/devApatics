Study Showed That 10% Of Workers Comp
Claims Paid Were Fraudulent

A 2021 APATICS analysis of over four (4) million workers compensation claims paid showed that 19,612 providers out of a network of 86,000 submitted fraudulent claims totaling about 10% of all claims paid during 2020. This study was conducted retrospectively using 2020 claims and network provider data for a regional workers compensation payer that paid about $107 million in fraudulent claims on approximately $1.17 billion in total payments.

APATICS deployed our Provider Network Integrity & Performance and FWA Detection, Prevention and Recovery solutions to retrospectively evaluate the integrity of claims that were already paid and to identify actions for recovery and prevention of future FWA activities. Each of the APATICS solutions used in this analysis are powered by our patented HALO AI™ intelligence platform.

The findings in this regional workers compensation study were similar to other analyses performed with the patented APATICS HALO AI™ technology platform. Most of the FWA activity identified retrospectively could have been detected and prevented in real-time using the Provider Network Integrity & Performance and FWA Detection, Prevention and Recovery solutions on a continuous basis.

Most of the FWA activity identified retrospectively could have been detected and prevented in real-time

Clean Providers Are The First Step In FWA Prevention

The first set of preventable FWA findings in this analysis were related to the provider network itself. Almost 23 percent (22.8%) of providers were flagged for some level of FWA activity. Twenty-two (22) providers were practicing with OIG, SAM or other health sanctions and should not have been in this or any other provider network.

Numerous providers were practicing with multiple tax ID numbers and hundreds of others had problematic collusion maps noting high-risk relationships with DME providers, pharmacies, or other healthcare professionals. All of these providers could have been identified and eliminated from the network before they were able to commit fraudulent, wasteful, or abusive actions with in-depth insight around their demographics, status, relationships, and behaviors.

Detect ‘Red Flag’ Behavioral And Transactional Risks Before FWA Occurs

The second set of preventable FWA findings center around coding and other detectable and preventable behavioral ‘red flag’ anomalies. Common findings included: 1) suspicious drug prescriptions with specific CPT codes, 2) outlier procedures associated with a particular ICD code or provider type and 3) procedures billed outside of evidence-based guidelines. All of these FWA-related anomalies and more are detectable and preventable in real time with HALO AI™ continuous surveillance that enables literally every claim to be audited against millions of complex combinations of CPT and ICD-10 codes and payer guidelines.

The study proved that this one regional payer can literally save tens of millions of dollars each year by mitigating FWA claims over-payments.

Quickly Recover FWA Claims Paid

APATICS FWA Detection, Prevention and Recovery Solution, in this analysis, proved to provide unprecedented intelligence for quantifying FWA activity by provider. Think of it as a complete FWA case file at the press-of-a-button.

This enables recovery efforts to begin quickly and equips collectors with deep provider-specific data to align recovery efforts as closely as possible with the action, creating the best possible scenario for collection.

Make Targeted Changes To Mitigate Future FWA Risk

This analysis demonstrated a number of macro-actions that the payer could take to mitigate future FWA risk. These steps included: 1) extract rendering provider information from PDF images, 2) manage invalid or missing data problems by back-walking based on known attributes, 3) improving the plan design and 4) improving formulary design.

Establish Real Time Detection & Prevention To Save Big Dollars

The workers compensation payer study demonstrated that detecting and preventing FWA claims from being paid, can literally save payers millions of dollars each year in the costs of over-payment and collection.

It also proved that the APATICS Provider Network Integrity & Performance and FWA Detection, Prevention & Recovery solutions are powerful tools in not only recovering FWA over-payments, but also in detecting and preventing the cost, time, and manpower burden of recovering fraudulent payments.

APATICS Appoints Clay Wilemon as President and Chief Commercial Officer

New Commercial Head Launched Over 500 New Healthcare Brands

SAN RAMON, CALIFORNIA (August 2, 2021) — APATICS, Inc. announced that Clay Wilemon has joined the company as President and Chief Commercial Officer. Clay will direct the healthcare data intelligence company’s commercial activities across healthcare payer, health systems, healthcare provider and government markets.

Clay is the former founder and CEO of DevicePharm, a strategic marketing firm that helped define and launch over 500 new healthcare brands. DevicePharm was acquired by Brado. In addition, Clay has been an active investor and director in healthcare technology companies and is a partner in Visionary Ventures. He is also a member of the Board of Directors at Octane, a Southern California economic development organization that has helped early-stage Orange County tech and med-tech companies raise over $2.8 billion in funding in the past decade.

Dimitri Arges, APATICS CEO said, “Clay’s skills in defining and launching new brands and solutions across the globe makes him a key part of our team at this time. APATICS is accelerating commercial growth with key healthcare data intelligence solutions that are validated and ready for broadscale commercial use. We are ready to help health insurance companies, third party administrators, healthcare providers and government healthcare agencies and programs reduce risk to immediately and continuously improve performance with easy access to data insights that will increase quality and reduce the cost of healthcare.”

Clay Wilemon added, “When I saw what APATICS was doing and the patented HALO AI™ intelligence platform that Theja Birur and Dimitri Arges had created, I knew that I wanted to be part of the team. APATICS is addressing over $1 trillion of healthcare market needs to improve provider data integrity and performance, dramatically reduce fraud, waste and abuse, and make the claims payment process work more efficiently for payers, providers and consumers.”
